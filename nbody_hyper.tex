\part{Formulation of the celestial n-body problem}

\section{From single to Jacobi to hyperspherical coordinates}

The intuitive way to specify a state of $N$ point masses $m_i$ without internal degrees of freedom--think of spin, color, flavor, isospin, \&c.--is to
assign three cartesian components for each such that the Hamiltonian reads
\be
\ham = \sum_{i=1}^N \frac{\ve{p}_i^2}{2m_i} + \sum_{i<j} V(\ve{r}_{ij}) = \sum_{i=1}^N \frac{\ve{p}_i^2}{2m_i} + \sum_{i<j} V(\ve{r}_i - \ve{r}_j)
\ee
\nomenclature{$\ve{r}_i$}{Psosition of particle $i$}
